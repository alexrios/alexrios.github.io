Speaking

I spend an unreasonable amount of time thinking about concurrency, memory management, and why your team's engineering process feels broken (spoiler: it's not the process).

Senior Staff Engineer at Stone Co, published author, and someone who's given technical talks on America, Asia, Africa, and Europe, explaining why the simplest solution is usually the one nobody wants to implement.

I've been talking about Go internals, Systems Programming, tech leadership that acknowledges human behavior instead of ignoring it, and system design patterns that survive contact with production. My approach: depth without density, pragmatism without cynicism, and explanations that work for engineers who've seen real codebases, not just toy examples.

Conference organizers typically invite me when they want someone who can make performance optimization or concurrency patterns accessible without dumbing them down, or when they need someone to say the quiet part out loud about engineering leadership.

Topics

System Programming, Go Internals & Performance (12+ talks)

Profile-Guided Optimization, Arenas, Generics, Memory Management, and the runtime internals that actually matter in production.

Concurrency Patterns (8+ talks)

Goroutines, coroutines comparison (Go vs Kotlin), synchronization primitives, and the practical reality of concurrent code.

Tech Leadership Reality (5+ talks)

Staff+ experiences, career progression, leading large-scale changes, and the parts nobody wants to admit are hard.

System Design & Engineering Practices (6+ talks)

High-performing team practices, architectural decisions, and system design patterns that survive production.

Developer Tools & Workflow (5+ talks)

Go tooling, build systems, dependency management, and productivity improvements that actually make a difference.
